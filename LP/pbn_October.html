Q: “How can pet parents keep their pets healthy and happy this fall?”

According to the experienced veterinarians at Scarbrough Animal Hospital in Sunrise, FL, autumn brings unique opportunities and challenges for pets. Here’s what pet parents should keep in mind this season:

1

Seasonal Nutrition & Weight
Adjust diets as pets become less active in cooler months. Consider high-quality, balanced meals to maintain healthy weight and energy.

2

Exercise & Outdoor Safety
Cooler weather is perfect for walks, but watch for fallen leaves, acorns, or allergens that can cause digestive or skin issues.

3

Dental & Immune Support
With holiday treats approaching, monitor snacks and maintain dental care routines to prevent oral issues and support immunity.

4

Parasite Prevention
Even in fall, ticks and fleas can still be active. Keep preventive treatments up to date.

5

Early Health Checks
Seasonal check-ins help detect early signs of arthritis, respiratory issues, or age-related conditions before colder months exacerbate them.

PAW-SOME LAUGHS: Your Monthly Dose of Pet Humor

Running a pet business can be a whirlwind, so here’s a little laughter to keep your spirits (and tails) high this October!

Why did the cat sit in front of the computer?

To keep an eye on his mouse! 🖱️🐾


When a client asks, “Will my dog behave around new friends?” and your four-legged guest immediately starts a game of tag with every toy in sight…

Organized chaos achieved! 🐕🎾


When a client says, “My cat loves quiet time,” and you enter to find them perched on your paperwork, staring like the boss of the office…

“Ah yes, very peaceful indeed.” 😼

Renowned attorney Roland Gomez had a fierce reputation in the courtroom — and a famous soft spot for dogs, especially Beagles and Basset Hounds.

One day, Roland “found” a Beagle wandering the parking lot outside his office. He told the staff the pup had “followed” him into the building… and somehow up to the fourth floor. Sure, Roland. Regardless of the logistics, the dog was quickly embraced by the team and given an official name: Partner — because, as Roland joked, he’d be the only partner in the firm.

Soon after came Judge, a rescued Basset Hound who also joined the daily office routine. Naturally, both dogs claimed their own chairs in Roland’s office and were considered part of the staff.

One day, before a deposition, opposing attorney Gerry D. took a seat in Roland’s office — unknowingly settling into Judge’s chair. As Partner curled up quietly in his own chair, Judge stared at Gerry with unwavering intensity. Flattered, Gerry thought he’d made a canine connection. Roland never had the heart to tell him Judge just wanted his chair back.

When Gerry stood up, the back of his suit was coated in Basset Hound fur. Roland politely gave him a heads-up… but kept the real reason for the stare-down to himself.

At the next deposition, Gerry returned — with his St. Bernard in tow. Apparently, the dogs-at-work policy had left an impression.

Best of all, the client being deposed — anxious and unsure — visibly relaxed the moment she saw the dogs. Roland even invited her to spend a few moments with them before things began. Just like that, a high-stress situation became a little more human… or perhaps, a little more dog.

Just another reminder that in the right paws, even legal drama can have a happy ending. I think we can all agree that dogs make everything better — even depositions.

Pet Care Boom Alert: The Billion-Dollar Surge No Pet Business Can Afford to Miss

A new Fortune Business Insights™ report reveals the global pet care market hit USD 259.37 billion in 2024 and is projected to soar to USD 427.75 billion by 2032, growing at a 6.6% CAGR. North America leads with a 33.6% market share, fueled by premiumization, wellness-focused spending, and rising pet ownership.

IMPACT: The growth highlights rising opportunities for pet businesses that focus on quality, sustainability, and digital engagement—meeting evolving expectations of modern pet owners.

Scenthound Secures Growth Investment to Expand U.S. Dog Wellness Franchise

Dog wellness franchise Scenthound has partnered with VMG Partners to fuel nationwide expansion of its membership-based model. The brand plans to scale rapidly across the U.S., emphasizing preventive grooming and wellness care for dogs of all breeds and sizes.

IMPACT: This move signals strong investor confidence in the “preventive pet wellness” model, opening doors for service providers to blend membership revenue with health-first offerings.

IBPSA Honors 2025 IBBY Award Winners for Pet Care Excellence

The International Boarding & Pet Services Association announced winners of its 2025 IBBY Awards, celebrating innovation, leadership, and community impact among pet care professionals. Recognized businesses are raising standards in operations, welfare, and customer experience

IMPACT: Awards like the IBBYs are helping define new benchmarks for professionalism in pet care—encouraging best practices that elevate the industry’s reputation and trust.

PetSmart Launches Search for Its Next “Chief Toy Tester”

PetSmart has kicked off applications for its Chief Toy Tester program, inviting one dog and one cat (plus their humans) to test and review upcoming toy releases. Winners will earn $10,000, exclusive perks, and play a creative role in product feedback.

IMPACT: A clever example of community-driven marketing—turning brand advocacy into a playful, high-engagement experience that humanizes retail.

Pet Obesity & Nutrition Survey 2025 Launches Nationwide

The Association for Pet Obesity Prevention (APOP) has opened its annual national survey, collecting data from veterinarians, pet owners, and industry professionals during World Pet Obesity Week (Oct 6–12). Results will shape 2026 awareness and education programs.

IMPACT: With 59% of U.S. dogs and 61% of cats overweight, this initiative drives the data needed to fuel healthier nutrition and fitness programs for pets.

Rooted Owl Expands Retail Footprint Across U.S.

Pet wellness brand Rooted Owl is expanding its U.S. retail presence through new distributor and specialty store partnerships. Known for its holistic, plant-based formulations, the brand is gaining momentum in the premium pet wellness market.

IMPACT: The rise of functional, ingredient-driven pet care reflects a consumer shift toward transparency and sustainability—creating space for new entrants in the “clean pet product” segment.

Subaru Commits $3.1 Million to Support Shelter Animals Nationwide

Subaru of America together with more than 630 participating retailers, has pledged over $3.1 million in grants , to local shelters through its Subaru Loves Pets® initiative with the ASPCA. The funds will help cover adoption fees, veterinary costs, and shelter improvements — giving more animals a chance to find loving homes.

IMPACT: Subaru’s initiative highlights how businesses can support shelters and strengthen community ties while promoting shared values of care and compassion..

Pet Retail Industry Report Highlights Key Growth Opportunities

A new report, State of the Industry: Pet Stores in the U.S., reveals that independent pet retailers are thriving by leaning into natural products, omnichannel selling, and millennial/Gen Z engagement strategies.

IMPACT: The findings underscore how personalization, sustainability, and digital transformation remain central to retail growth in 2025 and beyond.

DOING GOOD:
Alexandre Contreras & PetTrio Charity™

9

Stories That Pay It Forward—From Heart to Home

Every month, we spotlight an initiative that makes a tangible difference in animal welfare. This month’s champion: Alexandre Contreras and PetTrio Charity™.

Alexandre Contreras, a veterinary technician, innovator, and entrepreneur, founded PetTrio Charity™n 2022 to provide accessible veterinary care and support for pet owners in need. Based in Miami, Florida, this 501(c)(3) nonprofit enhances pet wellness through care, advocacy, and support.

The organization provides financial assistance to pet owners who cannot afford veterinary care, runs educational programs to inspire and develop future veterinary professionals, and partners with shelters, rescues, and other organizations to strengthen their work through funding, collaboration, and community initiatives.

Proceeds from KlipTrio® products help fund these veterinary services and educational programs, ensuring pets receive the care and guidance necessary for healthier, happier lives.

Through these initiatives, PetTrio Charity™ is making a significant impact on both animals and the communities that care for them.
