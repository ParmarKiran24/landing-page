Q: “How often should I bring my pet in for wellness exams, and what should I expect during these visits?”

According to the experienced veterinarians at Aardvark Animal Clinic—a clinic dedicated to comprehensive, compassionate pet care—regular wellness exams are essential to help pets thrive. Here’s what every pet parent should keep in mind:

1

Schedule Exams at Least Once a Year
Routine check-ups allow vets to detect early signs of illness and monitor growth, weight, and overall health.

2

Vaccinations & Preventive Care
Exams ensure vaccines are up to date and preventive measures like flea, tick, and heartworm protection are on track.

3

Dental & Oral Health Checks
Vets examine teeth and gums to prevent periodontal disease, which can affect overall health.

4

Nutrition & Weight Assessment
A balanced diet and maintaining a healthy weight are critical for energy, longevity, and disease prevention.

5

Behavior & Mobility Monitoring
Vets assess movement, joints, and behavior patterns to catch early signs of arthritis, anxiety, or other conditions.

6

Lab Tests When Needed
Bloodwork or urine tests may be recommended to monitor organ function, detect infections, or identify underlying issues before they become serious.

PAW-SOME LAUGHS: Your Monthly Dose of Pet Humor

Because running a pet business is a full-time adventure, a little laughter keeps the tails wagging and the spirits high!

What do you call a dog who loves science?

A “lab-radoratory assistant”!


When a client says, “My cat loves bath time,”

and you open the bathroom door to discover what looks like a tiny tornado of fur, claws, and soap bubbles…😂


When a client asks, “Will my dog get along with others?”

and your furry guest replies with a chewed-up shoe and a wagging tail… “Yes… but on my own terms!” 🐶👟

Kathy and her daughter Cheryl were dedicated volunteers for the Wildlife Care Center and especially loved fostering and rehabilitating birds. Every morning, the large dog crate they used as an aviary was moved outside to the backyard and opened so the birds could roam free. Oddly enough, the pigeons always went back into the crate on their own when evening rolled around.

Among the many pigeons they fostered, one in particular stood out because of her big personality and even bigger loyalty. Funny enough, they named her Pee Wee.

Pee Wee had a habit of walking across the piano keys while Kathy played, adding her own unique flair to every song. She also walked alongside Kathy as she mowed the lawn, like a pint-sized, feathery foreman—never missing a lap. And Pee Wee was extremely nosy. If the trash was being emptied, she perched on the garbage can, knowing Kathy or Cheryl would return to replace the bag.

One day, when a mother bird arrived with an injured wing, Kathy and Cheryl witnessed something magical: Pee Wee literally taught the mother bird how to fly again.

Cheryl, who had a special connection with birds, decided to bring Pee Wee to live with her about 15 miles away. Cheryl’s dog, Brownie—a gentle pit bull mix—also happened to love birds, so Pee Wee made herself right at home.
Until… a door was accidentally left open, and PeeWee flew out. Cheryl was devastated. She and Kathy cried for two weeks. They held onto hope that Pee Wee was out there living her best bird life—but deep down, they feared she was gone forever. Then one day, during some home repairs at Kathy’s house, a worker set up a ladder. And who landed right on it?

It was Pee Wee!

The repairman watched in confusion as Kathy screamed with joy and started talking to the pigeon like it was her long-lost friend. He thought she was absolutely nuts—until Pee Wee casually marched into the house like she’d never left. She had flown over 15 miles back to a place she had only known for a short time.

A few months later, it happened again—Pee Wee flew off from Cheryl’s. But this time, it only took her a couple of days to return to Kathy’s home. From that point on, Pee Wee decided that was her home. And that’s where she stayed for a couple of wonderful years—mentoring other birds, adding flair to piano recitals, and continuing her loyal companionship.

And then, one day, she left.
But this time, Kathy and Cheryl didn’t cry. They knew it was time. They believed Pee Wee found her mate, spread her wings, and finally claimed the wild, free life she was always meant to live.

Pee Wee was a musician, a mentor, and a funny, feathery sidekick—whose memory always brings a smile (and a giggle) to everyone who knew her.

COVET STRENGTHENS CLINICAL LEADERSHIP TO ADVANCE AI-DRIVEN VETERINARY CARE

CoVet has launched a dedicated Medical Department, integrating board-certified veterinarians and clinical experts into the core development of its AI copilot platform. This team, forming the foundation of CoVet’s Specialty Advisory Board, is ensuring that product innovation is guided by deep clinical insight, ethical rigor, and real-world usability—bridging the gap between advanced technology and the day-to-day realities of veterinary practice.

IMPACT: By embedding clinical leadership at the heart of product design, CoVet is setting a new standard for AI in veterinary care—enhancing trust, improving adoption, and ensuring tools align with the nuanced needs of veterinary teams and patients alike.

CORPORATE ECOSYSTEMS NOW INCLUDE PET CARE

Major employers are redefining workforce well-being by making pet care a core part of their benefits strategy. In the Washington D.C. metro, companies like Google, Adobe, and Lyft are rolling out pet insurance subsidies, virtual veterinary access, integrated wellness plans, and pet-accommodating office spaces. This move reflects more than cultural goodwill—it’s a structural shift. Platforms like Airvet have seen a 267% jump in regional users from 2023 to 2024, fueled by Gen Z and Millennial professionals who view pets as family.

IMPACT: As pet-inclusive benefits become a competitive differentiator in talent acquisition and retention, pet care providers have a growing opportunity to partner with corporate wellness programs, creating new revenue streams while strengthening employee engagement

2025 PET INNOVATION AWARDS SPOTLIGHT INDUSTRY GAME-CHANGERS

The annual Pet Innovation Awards have honored this year’s most groundbreaking products and services, reflecting the industry’s push toward smarter, more personalized pet care solutions. From science-backed supplements and AI-driven customer support to eco-conscious dental care and multifunctional pet gear, the winners showcase how innovation can elevate both pet wellness and owner experience.

IMPACT: These award-winning solutions signal a clear market shift toward products that combine convenience, customization, and measurable results—presenting pet businesses with fresh opportunities to differentiate, capture emerging demand, and future-proof their offerings.

PET GROOMING MARKET ON TRACK FOR MAJOR GROWTH

The U.S. pet grooming market is projected to reach $4.03 billion by 2032, sustaining an 8.4% CAGR. Rising demand for premium, personalized services—from breed-specific styling to spa-grade treatments—is driving the sector’s momentum, with subscription models and boutique add-ons boosting loyalty and spend.

IMPACT: The grooming sector is shifting toward lifestyle-driven services, where wellness, styling, and retail integration create stronger year-round customer engagement.

VETERINARY REGENERATIVE MEDICINE: THE NEXT BIG WAVE

The global veterinary regenerative medicine market is forecast to jump from $452.97 million in 2025 to $1.4 billion by 2034, at a robust 13.23% CAGR. Cutting-edge therapies like stem cell treatments and platelet-rich plasma are transforming outcomes for mobility, injury recovery, and chronic pain.

IMPACT: Early adoption of regenerative treatments is poised to define market leaders, as advanced therapies move from niche specialty to mainstream veterinary care.

ORIJEN TRAILS APP: REDEFINING PET WELLNESS THROUGH OUTDOOR ADVENTURE

Champion Petfoods’ ORIJEN brand helps pet owners take wellness outdoors through the ORIJEN Trails App, developed in partnership with the National Park Foundation. The app provides access to over 100 pet-friendly trails across U.S. national parks, offering trail recommendations, scenic highlights, and pet-friendly rest areas along the way. It also includes features to track calorie expenditure and mileage, making it easier for pets and their owners to monitor activity and enjoy healthy, adventurous outings together.

IMPACT: The ORIJEN Trails App helps pet businesses engage health-conscious clients while promoting wellness and outdoor activity. It creates opportunities for education, partnerships, and value-added services within the pet care ecosystem.

U.S COMPANION ANIMAL IMAGING MARKET: A NEW FRONTIER IN PET HEALTHCARE

The U.S. companion animal imaging market is projected to grow at a 6.19% CAGR through 2033, driven by urbanization, rising disposable incomes, and the increasing adoption of pet insurance. Advanced diagnostic tools such as X-rays, ultrasounds, CT, and MRI are no longer limited to specialty clinics and are becoming part of mainstream veterinary practice. This shift reflects how pet parents are redefining expectations for quality care and outcomes.

IMPACT: Early adoption of advanced imaging enables veterinary practices to strengthen client trust and retention, while insurance providers can expand coverage options for pet families. Across the broader pet industry, improved diagnostics raise standards of care, fostering opportunities for education, partnerships, and enhanced services throughout the pet care ecosystem.
